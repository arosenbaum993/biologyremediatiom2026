04

Three-Week Remediation Plan

Two parallel plans, one per course, scoped to 12 work sessions: Monday through Thursday, 9:00–12:00, for three weeks. Each session is a 3-hour block, segmented (not a single lecture) into a retrieval warm-up, a direct-instruction core, guided practice, and a checkpoint or item-analysis close. Both plans follow the same logic: the cohort is Below Target nearly everywhere, so Week 1 rebuilds the highest-leverage foundational domain, Week 2 attacks the heaviest strands and interleaves Week 1, and Week 3 integrates and runs a full simulated retest under conditions.

The 3-hour block (every session, both courses)
9:00–9:20 Spaced retrieval warm-up — mixed items from prior sessions. Retrieval is the intervention, not review. 9:20–10:25 Direct-instruction core — the session's target domain, concrete model before vocabulary. 10:25–10:35 Break. 10:35–11:35 Guided to independent practice — faded scaffolding, stimulus-based released items. 11:35–12:00 Checkpoint or error analysis — logged to the Sheets pipeline by GSE domain.
Biology — diagnostic reality

Of 12 cohort students, the large majority are Below Target in every domain. There is no single weak strand to patch; the deficit is course-wide. Sequencing follows GSE prerequisite structure: Cells underpins Genetics, which underpins Evolution and Classification. Ecology is the most self-contained and is used as the confidence re-entry point in Week 1.

Biology

12-Session Sequence · Mon–Thu, 9–12

Week 1 · Mon–Thu (S1–S4)

Cells & Ecology: rebuild the floor

Targets SB1 (Cells) and SB5 (Ecology). Ecology front-loaded as accessible re-entry; Cells as the prerequisite spine.
  • S1 Mon: Full diagnostic, ~24 released items, all 5 domains, DOK 1–3 (uses the longer block). Confirms per-student gaps; not for grades. PM half: Cell structure and function.
  • S2 Tue: Cellular transport, homeostasis, cell energy (photosynthesis vs respiration) at DOK 2. Close on a Cells checkpoint.
  • S3 Wed: Ecology: energy flow, biogeochemical cycles, population interactions. Stimulus-annotation protocol introduced.
  • S4 Thu: Cells + Ecology integration; mixed 16-item checkpoint, paired error analysis. Flex-group for Week 2 set here.
Week 2 · Mon–Thu (S5–S8)

Genetics & Evolution: the heaviest strands

Targets SB2/SB3 (Cellular Genetics & Heredity) and SB6 (Evolution), the two domains with the deepest cohort Below counts.
  • S5 Mon: DNA structure, replication, protein synthesis as a cause-effect chain, anchored to Week 1 Cells.
  • S6 Tue: Inheritance, Punnett squares, pedigrees, DOK 2–3. Worked example to faded practice across the full block.
  • S7 Wed: Mechanisms of evolution: selection, drift, speciation, lines of evidence. Data-interpretation items.
  • S8 Thu: Interleaved retrieval (Cells, Ecology, Genetics, Evolution); cumulative checkpoint. Individual conferences with the 3–4 lowest.
Week 3 · Mon–Thu (S9–S12)

Classification & full integration

Targets SB4 (Classification & Phylogeny), the lightest EOC weight, then full simulated retest.
  • S9 Mon: Classification, cladograms, phylogeny as pattern reading tied to evolution evidence. PM half: stimulus-load test strategy (passage and graphic attack, elimination, time budgeting).
  • S10 Tue: Full-length simulated EOC, all domains, timed under conditions. Sheets-logged for item analysis.
  • S11 Wed: Targeted reteach driven by S10 item analysis; small flexible groups by weakest domain.
  • S12 Thu: Second short simulation on the weakest 2 domains; exit data, MTSS escalation flags, parent/teacher summary.

Grouping & delivery
  • Run S1, S4, S8, S10 whole-cohort; flex-group S3, S7, S11 by S1 and S8 data.
  • Pair students Below in all 5 domains with a single recovery teacher for the core block; do not distribute them across groups.
  • The 3-hour block lets diagnostic (S1) and simulation (S10) run full-length under realistic conditions in one sitting — use that, do not split tests across days.
Progress monitoring
  • Three checkpoints (S4, S8, S10) logged to the existing Sheets pipeline; report % correct by GSE domain, not raw score.
  • Movement target: each student advances at least one domain category (Below to Approaching, or Approaching to Met) on the S10 simulation.
  • Students still Below in 4+ domains at S10 escalate to MTSS Tier 3 with a documented post-window plan.
US History — diagnostic reality

The cohort is only 3 students, and all 3 are Below Target in 4 of 5 domains; New Republic through Reconstruction is the single domain where one student reaches Approaching. With this n and a 3-hour block, the plan is a single tutorial-intensity group, chronologically anchored: the GSE strands are sequential, so rebuilding the timeline is the intervention. The small n is an asset here — every session can run as conferenced, individualized instruction.

US History

12-Session Sequence · Mon–Thu, 9–12

Week 1 · Mon–Thu (S1–S4)

Foundations: Colonization to Reconstruction

SSUSH1–10. The two earliest domains; without this spine the later domains cannot be reasoned through.
  • S1 Mon: Full diagnostic across all 5 domains (uses the long block). Each student starts a personal timeline skeleton maintained the entire window. PM half: Colonization through the Constitution — causation chains, founding documents.
  • S2 Tue: New Republic through Civil War: expansion, sectionalism, cause vs effect.
  • S3 Wed: Reconstruction; stimulus-based items with a source-analysis protocol.
  • S4 Thu: Domains 1–2 checkpoint; timeline check-in and individual conferences (feasible with n=3).
Week 2 · Mon–Thu (S5–S8)

Industrialization to World Power

SSUSH11–18. Industrialization, Reform & Imperialism, then Establishment as a World Power.
  • S5 Mon: Industrialization, immigration, Progressive reform.
  • S6 Tue: Imperialism and WWI as cause-effect, tied back to Week 1 expansion themes.
  • S7 Wed: 1920s, Depression, New Deal; political cartoon and data-stimulus practice.
  • S8 Thu: WWII and emergence as a world power. Interleaved retrieval across Domains 1–4; cumulative checkpoint, conferences with all 3.
Week 3 · Mon–Thu (S9–S12)

Post-WWII & full integration

SSUSH19–25, then simulated retest across the full course.
  • S9 Mon: Cold War, Civil Rights, modern era to present. PM half: document and stimulus strategy — sourcing, elimination, time budgeting.
  • S10 Tue: Full-length simulated EOC, timed under conditions, Sheets-logged.
  • S11 Wed: Targeted reteach from S10 item analysis, individualized.
  • S12 Thu: Second short simulation on weakest domains; named individual exit summaries.
Grouping & delivery
  • Single tutorial group of 3. One teacher, full window, for continuity of the timeline build.
  • The running personal timeline is the through-line artifact; every domain hangs on it. Do not teach domains as isolated units.
  • With n=3 and a 3-hour block, every session is effectively 1:1 conferenced — exploit it; do not run this as whole-group lecture.
Progress monitoring
  • Checkpoints S4, S8, S10 logged by SSUSH strand, reported as % correct by domain.
  • Movement target: every student advances at least one domain category by S10; given the n, name students individually in the exit summary.
  • Any student still Below in 3+ domains at S10 moves to a documented MTSS Tier 3 plan.
